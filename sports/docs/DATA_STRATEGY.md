# Public-link data strategy

The site should be a trusted gateway, not a scraper.

## Principles

1. Link to official sporting bodies and event hubs.
2. Store short curated summaries and tags only.
3. Attribute each source by name and URL.
4. Keep `last_verified` dates on records.
5. Avoid copying fixtures, rankings, tables or player-stat databases unless the source explicitly allows reuse.
6. Give sporting bodies a clear way to request corrections or updates.

## Priority sources

- National sporting body directories.
- International federation sites.
- Official event/tournament pages.
- Inclusion and participation resources from recognised sport agencies.
- Disability and Para sport pathway organisations.

## Commercial caution

Before turning on sponsorship or ad networks, publish a sponsor policy. For a participation-focused site, reject ads that clash with the mission, especially gambling-heavy creative, misleading supplements, unsafe training claims, or content that makes sport feel exclusionary.
