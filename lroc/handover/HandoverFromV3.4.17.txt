LROC Handover — v3.4.17 Article Preview Alignment / Page-Break Cleanup

Baseline: v3.4.16 had the article rendering close, with captions working and manual image movement available, but the Article Library A4 layout preview was still using an older vertical-scroll approximation. That made page thumbnails disagree with the Issue Builder renderer: the gearbox image and FPR image appeared on the wrong preview pages, and a blank third preview page could still appear.

Changes made in v3.4.17:
- The Article Library A4 layout preview now calls the same TinyMCE article pagination/window renderer used by Issue Builder placement.
- Preview pages are rendered as horizontal clipped windows across the same two-column article sheet, not vertical translateY slices.
- Preview page count comes from the renderer's meaningful page windows.
- Old TinyMCE/manual page-break `<hr>` markers are stripped during Article Library HTML normalisation.
- The TinyMCE pagebreak plugin/toolbar item and the custom Blocks menu manual page-break item were removed, because the markers were visually misleading and could force phantom pages.
- Caption handling from v3.4.13 is retained.
- Manual image earlier/later flow tools from v3.4.15 are retained.
- The LROC Issue Builder footer style is retained.

Testing focus:
1. Open the TD5 gearbox article in Article Library and verify the A4 preview matches the Issue Builder placement much more closely.
2. Confirm the gearbox image appears in the expected top-right area of page 1 after manual image placement changes.
3. Confirm the FPR image appears on page 2 where expected.
4. Confirm no blank/phantom page 3 appears when the article genuinely finishes on page 2.
5. Confirm captions still edit on double-click and render correctly.
6. Re-place the article fresh after deployment so old page metadata and old page-break markers do not remain in placed issue pages.

Known caveat:
- The Article Library preview is still browser-rendered HTML, not the final PDF exporter, but it now uses the same renderer path as Issue Builder article placement rather than the old approximation.
